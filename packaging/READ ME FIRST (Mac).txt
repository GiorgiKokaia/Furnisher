FURNISHER — how to open it on a Mac
===================================

What this is: a little program that helps you furnish an apartment. You pick or
draw a floor plan, then just tell it what you want in plain words ("furnish the
bedroom, cosy, budget 1500") and it fills the rooms with real furniture that
actually fits, and shows you photos of how it would look.

You need: a Mac and an internet connection. There is nothing to install.


STEP 1 — Unzip
--------------
Double-click the zip you received. You'll get two files, including
"Furnisher.command". Drag them onto your Desktop so they're easy to find.


STEP 2 — The important bit: the FIRST time, don't double-click. Right-click.
---------------------------------------------------------------------------
Macs block programs that didn't come from the App Store, and if you just
double-click you'll get a dead-end message like "Apple could not verify...".
To get around it the first time:

   1. RIGHT-click (or hold Control and click) "Furnisher.command"
   2. Choose "Open" from the little menu
   3. A warning appears — this time it has an "Open" button. Click it.

You only have to do this once. After that you can open it normally.


STEP 3 — Wait about 20 seconds
------------------------------
A Terminal window with text in it will appear. Ignore what it says, but LEAVE
IT OPEN — that window IS the program. Closing it turns the program off.

Your web browser should open by itself with Furnisher in it. If it doesn't,
look in the Terminal window for a line like:

   http://127.0.0.1:8380

and type that into your browser yourself.


STEP 4 — Use it
---------------
- "Start from scratch" is the quickest way in: pick a room type and go.
- Or pick one of the ready-made apartments and hit "Furnish".
- Then type what you want in the chat box on the right, for example:
    set a budget of 1500
    furnish the bedroom, scandinavian, cosy
- It offers you a few options — click the one you like and it places the
  furniture on the plan.
- Click a piece of furniture to see what it is, move it, or swap it.
- The camera buttons make photos of the finished room.


WHEN YOU'RE DONE
----------------
Close the Terminal window. Everything you made is saved and will still be
there next time you open it.


IF STEP 2 STILL WON'T LET IT OPEN
---------------------------------
Some newer Macs are extra strict. If right-click -> Open still dead-ends:

   1. Open the "Terminal" app (press Cmd+Space, type Terminal, hit Enter)
   2. Type this exactly, then drag "Furnisher.command" onto the window and
      press Enter:
         xattr -cr
      (there should be a space after -cr, then the file you dragged)
   3. Now double-click Furnisher.command normally.
